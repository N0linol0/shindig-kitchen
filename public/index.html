Crispy Smashed Potatoes
with Herby Vegan Ranch

Shatteringly crispy smashed potatoes tossed with a bright, herb-packed vegan dressing and fresh cucumber. A side dish that tends to disappear fast.

Units
Servings
15 minPrep
55 minCook
70 minTotal
Ingredients
Potatoes
Baby Yukon Gold potatoes14 oz
Olive oil sprayas needed
Salt & cracked black pepperto taste
Salad
Persian cucumber, deseeded & diced1
Small red onion, finely chopped1
Dressing
Vegan Greek yogurt¼ cup
Vegan mayonnaise½ cup
Garlic clove, minced1
Dijon mustard1 tbsp
Wholegrain mustard1 tbsp
Fresh dill, chopped¼ cup
Flat-leaf parsley, chopped3 tbsp
Chives, chopped3 tbsp
Cracked black pepper¼ tsp
Lemon, juiced1
Method
1
Boil the potatoes
Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Add the potatoes and cook until completely fork-tender, about 10–15 minutes depending on size. You want them soft all the way through — they'll crisp up in the oven. Drain and let steam-dry for a few minutes.
2
Prep the salad
While the potatoes cook, finely dice the cucumber and chop the red onion. Set aside in a bowl — you'll dress them later.
3
Make the dressing
Whisk together the yogurt, mayo, garlic, both mustards, dill, parsley, chives, pepper, and lemon juice until smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust salt as needed. Set aside — the flavors will keep developing as it sits.
4
Smash and season
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Arrange the drained potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet. Using the flat bottom of a drinking glass, press each potato down firmly to about ¼ inch (6 mm) thick. Slide the glass off in a flat motion to avoid sticking. Spray generously with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, then spray once more.
5
Roast until craggly and golden
Roast for 40–50 minutes until deeply golden, crispy at the edges, and craggly on top. Don't rush this — the color is the flavor. No need to flip.
6
Rest the potatoes, dress the salad
Pull the potatoes from the oven and let them cool for 10 minutes — this keeps them crispy and stops the dressing from turning watery. While they rest, toss the cucumber and onion into the dressing bowl and combine.
7
Toss and serve
Add half the rested potatoes to the salad and toss gently — they'll break into uneven chunks, which is exactly what you want. Pile into a serving dish and scatter the remaining crispy potatoes over the top for texture. Serve straight away.
Cook's notes
Vegan by design, but works equally well with regular Greek yogurt and mayo if that's what you have. The dressing is also excellent as a dip for crudités or a spread on sandwiches — make a double batch and thank yourself later.
